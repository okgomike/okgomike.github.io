      What is the difference between stainless steel 304 and galvanized hose clamps?
    answer: >-
      Stainless steel 304 clamps offer superior corrosion resistance and are ideal for outdoor, marine, and high-moisture environments. Galvanized steel clamps are more economical and suitable for dry indoor applications. Zinc plated options provide intermediate corrosion protection at a moderate price.
  - question: >-
      How do I select the correct hose clamp size?
    answer: >-
      Measure the outer diameter of the hose when it is fully installed on the fitting. Select a clamp whose adjustable range covers this measurement. For example, if your hose OD is 25mm, choose the 16-27mm or 21-44mm clamp depending on the required clamping force.
  - question: >-
      Can these clamps be reused after removal?
    answer: >-
      Yes, worm drive hose clamps are fully reusable. The screw mechanism allows for repeated adjustment and removal without damaging the clamp. However, inspect the band for fatigue or distortion before reusing in critical applications.
  - question: >-
      What torque should I apply when tightening hose clamps?
    answer: >-
      Tighten the clamp until the hose is snug against the fitting with no visible gaps, typically 3-5 inch-pounds for standard automotive and plumbing applications. Over-tightening can damage the hose, while under-tightening may cause leaks. Use a torque screwdriver for precision in critical applications.
