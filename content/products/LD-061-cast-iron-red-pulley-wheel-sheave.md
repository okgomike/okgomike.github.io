  - question: "What is the difference between single and double sheave pulleys?"
    answer: "A single sheave pulley has one wheel and changes the direction of the rope while providing a basic mechanical advantage. A double sheave pulley has two wheels on the same frame and can provide a greater mechanical advantage (typically 2:1 or 3:1 depending on the rigging configuration) by allowing multiple rope passes. Double sheave pulleys are used when heavier loads need to be lifted with less effort."
  - question: "How do I select the correct pulley wheel diameter for my application?"
    answer: "The pulley diameter should be selected based on the rope diameter and the load requirements. As a general rule, the pulley diameter should be at least 10 to 20 times the rope diameter for optimal rope life. For light-duty applications like clotheslines, 2-3 inch pulleys are suitable. For heavier lifting applications, 4-6 inch pulleys provide better rope support and durability."
