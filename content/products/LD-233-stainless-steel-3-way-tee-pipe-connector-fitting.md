  - question: "What is the difference between equal and reducing tee fittings?"
    answer: "Equal tee fittings have all three ports at the same diameter, used when all connecting pipes are the same size. Reducing tee fittings have a larger main run (two opposing ports) and a smaller branch outlet, used when connecting a smaller pipe to a larger main pipe run. The reducing tee is also useful for adding support members to a framework."
  - question: "How do I secure the pipes into the tee fitting?"
    answer: "The pipes can be secured using set screws through pre-drilled holes in the fitting, construction adhesive or epoxy applied inside the fitting bore, or TIG welding for permanent installations.
