  - question: "What is the difference between taper, plug, and bottoming taps?"
    answer: "Taper taps have a long lead that starts threads gradually, ideal for starting blind holes. Plug taps have a shorter lead for general through-hole tapping. Bottoming taps have a very short lead for threading the bottom of blind holes. The LD-200 set includes plug taps suitable for most applications."
  - question: "How do I determine the correct drill size for tapping?"
    answer: "The correct tap drill size is approximately the thread diameter minus the thread pitch. For M6x1.0: 6 - 1.0 = 5mm drill. For M8x1.25: 8 - 1.25 = 6.8mm drill. Using the correct drill size prevents tap breakage and ensures proper thread engagement."
  - question: "How do I prevent tap breakage?"
    answer: "Use the correct drill size for the tap. Apply cutting fluid generously. Turn the tap forward 1/4 turn, then reverse 1/8 turn to break chips. Never force the tap. Keep the tap aligned perpendicular to the workpiece. Clear chips frequently by fully withdrawing the tap."
  - question: "Can these taps and dies be used on stainless steel?"
    answer: "Yes, M2 HSS taps and dies can cut threads in stainless steel, but it requires more care. Use sulfur-based cutting oil, slower speed, and frequent chip clearing. Pre-drill at the upper end of the recommended drill size range for stainless steel to reduce torque."
